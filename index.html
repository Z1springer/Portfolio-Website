This project was done for my Data Capstone class
in order to complete my Associates Degree.

The degree is for Data Analytics and Software Development which I hope will help in me getting a job in this field.

This project was undertaken to assist students in the UAE in a Data Analysis of ecological survey data.

One of my primary tasks was to search for outliers in the data. Using a data visualization tool called Tableau, I was able to accomplish this easily. While not the most complicated of tasks, challenges and difficulties still reared up and were overcome. The link to my results is below.

First Objective

Send out the survey to those close to me. Gather the results of each survey and compile them. Once the data is in one place I must clean the data to make each entry more uniform.

Second Objective

Meat with my Project Team to discuss what ecological questions can be answered by our combined data. From this meating, decide who will focus on which question and subsequent data visualizations.

Third Objective

Using data visualization tools, identify key points of interest. Making clear what aspects of the data were used. And pass along the results to our counterparts in the UAE

Some Obsticles I ran into are as follows

Procrastination

A common predicament run into by everyone. And one I struggle with every day that I continuously work to combat. Breaking down each task into smaller pieces helps immensely, but that still leaves the need to fight against the urges that my ADD always puts in the way of progress.

Imposter Syndrome

This has been a constant in my conscious thoughts since I began pursuing my degree. Always questioning if this path is one that I am capable of walking, but I have since found the support structure that I need to be able to cope and grow past such thoughts.

Some Reflections and Thoughts of Improvment I have on the Project

Possible Improvments

While there was plenty of guidence and assistance on the part of my instructors. There were still many instances of feeling lost and confused. Having more structure and clear set goals in terms of what must be answered and what means to use to visualize these answers would be reassuring. When left to my own devices and allowed to determine the parameters of what my project entails, I often overthink the simplest of tasks, which leads to a lack of progress.

Reflections

During the course of this class, we learned of the Agile Mindset. Though I cannot say that my project group and I utilized many of the aspects of it. The Stand-Up meetings were the most useful part to help keep each member of the team appraised on what each of us are working on at the time. The project itself however did not seem to have a clear target audience beyond our counterparts in the UAE. I however learned much about both the Data Analysis process and what I need to improve going forward in this specific field of work. There were many aspects to this project that I had not come into contact with priar to this class, and I was glad to be illuminated on the many pieces it takes to make even a small Data Analysis.
